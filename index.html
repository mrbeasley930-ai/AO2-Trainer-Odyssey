OCR Classical Civilisation H408/21 — The World of the Hero

What the examiner is really rewarding.

Look at any OCR mark scheme and you see the same shape repeated. Each bullet of indicative content is an analytical point (AO2) with a precise quotation or reference nested underneath it (AO1). Knowledge alone caps you in the lower levels; the marks climb when every point you make is anchored to the text and pushed on to why it matters.

The chain you are training:

FEATURE  →  EFFECT  →  SIGNIFICANCE

Name the feature (an epic simile, a formulaic epithet, a moment of xenia, dramatic irony), explain its effect on Homer's audience, then link it to a theme or argument running through the whole poem. That third move — significance — is what separates a top-band answer.

The five trainers:

Spot the Technique — read a passage, identify the literary or cultural feature at work.
Point → Evidence — match an AO2 claim to the exact quotation that proves it (the mark-scheme move).
Build the AO2 Chain — assemble feature, effect and significance, then magpie the model sentence.
Write It Yourself — given the feature and quotation, write your own effect and significance, then check your draft against a model answer and the AO2 descriptors. This is the step that turns recognition into the writing the exam demands.
Magpie Bank — flashcards of the analytical vocabulary and ready-made sentence stems to steal.
